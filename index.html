MILE-Tac Hand Modular Isomorphic Linker with Sub-Degree Sensing and Tactile Integration for Dexterous Manipulation Demonstration Collection.

Abstract

The system integrates fingertip visuotactile sensing with a mechanically isomorphic MILE exoskeleton to collect dexterous hand demonstrations.

It achieves sub-degree joint accuracy and sub-millimetre teleoperation redirection precision, enabling complex, contact-rich in-hand manipulation. A modular, low-cost tactile design provides high-resolution contact measurements, while synchronized vision and tactile streams are logged for learning. Policies trained on the collected data with visuotactile inputs outperform vision-only baselines on contact-rich tasks, indicating improved robustness and inference quality.
